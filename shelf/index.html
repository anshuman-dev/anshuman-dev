Things I recommend

Books, articles, and other things worth your time.

  • Last Lecture Series: How to Live your Life at Full Power ↗

    Graham Weaver on living and working with full intensity: a talk worth revisiting.
  • Galaxy brain resistance ↗

    Vitalik Buterin on "galaxy brain resistance": how much a way of thinking can be twisted to justify any conclusion, and why that's a useful test for how much to trust an argument.
  • 95%-ile isn't that good ↗

    Dan Luu on why reaching a respectable percentile in most skills doesn't take talent: it takes actually noticing and fixing your basic mistakes, which most people never do.
  • The Brand Age ↗

    Paul Graham on how Swiss watchmaking shifted from engineering excellence to brand and status once substantive differences between products vanished, and why it's better to chase interesting problems than brands.
  • Welcome to the Room ↗

    Jeffrey Snover recounts Satya Nadella's blunt advice to newly promoted Microsoft execs: manufacture success with the resources you're given, don't manage decline with excuses.
  • Finite and Infinite Mindsets ↗

    On holding a finite-game mindset (win now) and an infinite-game mindset (leave things better for the next generation) at the same time.
  • 21 Lessons From 14 Years at Google ↗

    Addy Osmani on what actually separates thriving engineers: not raw coding skill, but navigating people, priorities, and everything around the code.
  • Nested Learning ↗

    Arjun Kocher on nested learning.
